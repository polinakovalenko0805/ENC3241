# Proposal

## Enhancing Student Safety Through a Location-Based Campus Safety Application

## Background
College campuses are generally considered safe, but students still face risks such as walking alone at night or dealing with emergency situations. Improving awareness and access to safety resources is an important part of reducing these risks. Campus safety initiatives often rely on technology and awareness to reduce risks and improve student response to emergencies (U.S. Department of Education, 2020). However, many students still report feeling unsafe in certain situations, especially when walking alone at night or in unfamiliar areas.

With the widespread use of smartphones today, mobile applications can play an important role in improving personal safety. Research shows that smartphone devices are a promising self-management tool for depression (Firth et al., 2017), suggesting that mobile technology can influence user behavior and well-being. Additionally, smartphone usage is extremely common among young adults. According to Pew Research Center, 81% of adults now own a smartphone (Anderson, 2019), highlighting how accessible mobile-based solutions are.

However, many existing safety applications are not tailored to the specific needs of college students. This project focuses on understanding what students actually want from a campus safety application before designing one.

## Methodology
This project will focus on survey-based research to better understand student needs and preferences.

- Develop a survey targeting 50–75 college students  
- Distribute the survey online (e.g., Google Forms) and through campus organizations  
- Survey topics will include:
  - Perceived safety on campus  
  - Situations where students feel unsafe  
  - Current safety behaviors  
  - Desired features in a campus safety app  
- Collect responses over a 1–2 week period  
- Analyze results using:
  - Percentages and trends  
  - Comparison between responses  

## Anticipated Outcomes
The project is expected to:

- Identify common safety concerns among students  
- Reveal patterns in student behavior and awareness  
- Determine which safety app features students value the most  
- Provide data that can guide future development of a campus safety application  

## Significance

**Use of the project:**  
This research applies user-centered design principles by prioritizing real user needs before building a solution.

**Intended audience:**
- College students  
- University administration  
- Software developers  

**Project Benefits:**
- Provides data-driven insight into student safety concerns  
- Supports better design of campus safety tools  
- Can be used for future app development or research  
- Results may be presented at a student research conference
